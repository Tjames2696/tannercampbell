The Power of Intuition

By Tanner Campbell February 1, 2026 6 min read

Intuition often gets dismissed as irrational—that mysterious gut feeling that defies logic. But neuroscience reveals something different: intuition is your brain's way of rapidly processing vast amounts of information.

Your subconscious mind observes patterns, contextual clues, and subtle signals that your conscious mind hasn't consciously registered. This happens at lightning speed, and the result surfaces as a feeling, a hunch, or an instinct.

The most successful decision-makers don't ignore their intuition or blindly follow it. Instead, they respect it as data. They notice what their gut is telling them, investigate why they feel that way, and use that information alongside analytical thinking.

Learning to trust your intuition isn't about becoming less rational. It's about integrating both modes of thinking. Listen to that quiet signal. Ask yourself why you're feeling it. Then decide consciously, informed by both your analytical mind and your intuitive wisdom.
